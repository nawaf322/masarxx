Business Model

Understanding the problem Deprixa Plus solves, the target market it serves, and the commercial model behind it.

The Problem

Small to medium courier and logistics companies typically start with a combination of spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, physical logbooks, and disconnected tools to manage their operations. As the business grows, this approach creates serious operational problems:

No Centralized Tracking

Shipment status lives in someone's head or a shared spreadsheet. Customers have no self-service tracking. Status updates require manual phone calls.

Billing Chaos

Invoices are created manually in Word or Excel, sent inconsistently, and rarely tracked to payment. Revenue leaks from uninvoiced shipments.

No Accountability

When a package is lost or delayed, there is no audit trail to understand what happened. Disputes are resolved by guessing, not by data.

Zero Visibility

Managers cannot see KPIs in real time. How many shipments are out for delivery right now? What is the exception rate this week? The data exists — it just isn't accessible.

Branch Isolation

Multiple branches operate independently with no shared data. Head office cannot see branch performance without manually requesting reports by email.

Security Risk

Shared login credentials, no role-based access, and no audit log. Any employee can see all client data, pricing, and financial information.

Deprixa Plus solves all of these problems by providing a single, integrated platform purpose-built for the courier and logistics industry.

License Terminology

A Regular License is for use in a single project where end users are not charged for access. An Extended License is required if you are charging end users, reselling as a service, or removing branding for a client. Contact us for Enterprise multi-site deals.

Revenue Streams

License Sales

Primary revenue from one-time license purchases via Envato/CodeCanyon or direct sales. Customers pay once and receive 12 months of updates and forum support.

  • Regular License: single project use
  • Extended License: SaaS/reselling use
  • Enterprise: custom pricing, multi-install

Support Renewals

Annual support and update renewal subscriptions. After the first 12 months, customers can renew at a reduced rate to continue receiving updates and accessing the support channel.

  • 6-month renewal: access to new releases
  • 12-month renewal: releases + priority support
  • Managed hosting add-on: optional

White-Label Reselling

Extended license holders (agencies, consultants, SaaS operators) can brand and resell Deprixa Plus to their own clients. This multiplies revenue without additional development cost.

  • Customize branding, colors, and logo
  • Bill clients monthly for hosting + support

Customer Onboarding Flow

From purchase to live operation, a typical customer journey takes 30 minutes to 4 hours depending on technical expertise and data migration requirements.

  1. 1

    Purchase

    Customer purchases a license on Envato/CodeCanyon or directly. They receive a purchase code (license key) and a download link for the source code ZIP file.

  2. 2

    Download & Deploy

    Customer extracts the ZIP file to their VPS or web server. They follow the Installation Guide to run composer, npm, and database migrations. Technical customers take 15–30 minutes. Non-technical customers may purchase installation assistance.

  3. 3

    Activate License

    Customer logs in and navigates to Settings > Updates. They enter their purchase code to activate the license via the license server. Activation links the installation to the purchase, enables update checking, and unlocks the full feature set.

  4. 4

    Configure Company Profile

    Customer sets their company name, logo, contact details, country, currency, and timezone in Settings > Company Profile and Settings > Locale. This takes 5–10 minutes.

  5. 5

    Add Users & Branches

    Customer creates user accounts for their team members and assigns appropriate roles (Admin, Employee, Driver, etc.). They also create Branch records for each physical location. Users can be invited via email.

  6. 6

    Configure Services & Pricing

    Customer sets up their service types (Standard, Express, Same-Day, Freight) with pricing rules. They configure shipment status workflows and notification templates to match their operational process.

  7. 7

    Go Live

    Customer begins creating real shipments. Staff log in using their assigned credentials. Customers receive their tracking links. The system is fully operational. Ongoing: manage shipments, generate invoices, run reports, monitor the dashboard.
